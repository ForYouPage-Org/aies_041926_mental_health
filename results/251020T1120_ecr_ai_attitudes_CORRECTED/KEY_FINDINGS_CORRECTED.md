## 🧠 Psychological Interpretation

### Why Anxiety Predicts AI Attitudes

**Attachment Theory Framework:**

1. **Hyperactivating Strategy**

   - Anxious individuals intensify proximity-seeking
   - ChatGPT provides **available, non-rejecting "other"**
   - No risk of abandonment or rejection

2. **Emotional Regulation Function**

   - AI offers **validation without judgment**
   - Provides **comfort and reassurance on demand**
   - Reduces anxiety through availability

3. **Safe Haven**
   - AI serves **secure base function**
   - Always accessible for emotional support
   - Never withdraws or becomes unavailable

### Why Avoidance Shows Limited Effects

**Deactivating Strategy:**

1. **Emotional Suppression**

   - Avoidant individuals **minimize attachment needs**
   - Less likely to seek emotional support (even from AI)
   - Intellectual/task focus predominates

2. **Exception: Disclosure**

   - AI allows **disclosure without intimacy threat**
   - No risk of emotional entanglement
   - Maintains emotional distance while sharing

3. **Instrumental Use**
   - Likely use AI for tasks, not relationships
   - Avoids emotional engagement with AI (and humans)

## 🎯 Key Takeaways for Publication

### Abstract-Level Summary

"Using corrected 5-point AI attitude scales, we found **attachment anxiety significantly predicted 9 of 15 AI attitudes** (N=212), with strongest effects for disclosure preference (r=.44), emotional relief (r=.32), and feeling understood (r=.31). **Attachment avoidance predicted only 2 attitudes**, primarily disclosure. Multiple regression showed **anxiety as the dominant predictor** (β=.27-.53), explaining up to 21% of variance in AI attitudes. Results suggest **anxiously attached individuals are particularly vulnerable to AI dependency** and use AI to fulfill attachment functions."

### Main Conclusions

1. **Attachment anxiety drives emotional AI use**
2. **Avoidance shows limited, specific effects**
3. **AI serves attachment functions for vulnerable users**
4. **Dependency concerns warranted for anxious individuals**
5. **Need for attachment-informed AI design**
